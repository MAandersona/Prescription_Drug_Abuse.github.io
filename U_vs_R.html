Urban Perspective

  • Central Nervous System Depressants (Benzos/ Benzodiazepines)
  • Hallucinogens
  • Heroin (Diamorphine)
  • Inhalants
  • Opioid analgesics
  • Sedatives
  • Tranquilizers
  • Stimulants
  • Painkillers

Although prescription drug abuse affects many Americans, certain populations, such as youth, old adults and women may be at particular risk. Abuse of prescription drugs is highest among the youth adults aged 18 to 25 with 5.9 percent reporting nonmedical use in the past month. (https://nida.nih.gov).

Preventative measures for Prescription drug abuse

Creating awareness about the side effects and consequences of the addiction, Treatment of the people who are already addicted being provided, avoiding addiction to these drugs and substances, providing moral support and counselling to those that are already addicts, avoiding any kinds of temptations and peer pressure, never stopping or changing a dose without first discussing it with a doctor, avoiding the use of other people's prescriptions and never giving one's personal prescription to others may be some of the best ways we can stop prevent the abuse of these drugs. Many rural areas in the countries haven't yet been able to advance because some areas are unreachable to the local and government services to treat or to allow to be built for better safety of the environment.

Rural residents often encounter barriers to healthcare that limit their ability to obtain the care they need. Access to healthcare implies that healthcare services are available and obtainable in a timely manner. Yet rural residents often encounter barriers to healthcare access. Even when an adequate supply of healthcare services exists in the community, there are other factors that may impede healthcare access. For instance, to have healthcare access, rural residents must also have:

  • Financial means to pay for services, such as health or dental insurance that is accepted by the provider
  • Means to reach and use services, such as transportation to services that may be located at a distance, and the ability to take paid time off of work to use such services
  • Confidence in their ability to communicate with healthcare providers, particularly if the patient is not fluent in English or has limited health literacy
  • Trust that they can use services without compromising privacy
  • Confidence that they will receive quality care.

prevention

Preventing prescription drug abuse in rural areas of countries like Kenya is an important public health concern. Some strategies that have been implemented in Kenya to prevent prescription drug abuse include:

  • Strengthening the regulation of prescription drugs: In Kenya, the Pharmacy and Poisons Board is responsible for regulating the distribution and sale of prescription drugs. This includes issuing licenses to pharmacies and drug wholesalers, setting standards for the storage and handling of prescription drugs, and monitoring the distribution and sale of controlled substances.
  • Increasing access to addiction treatment: In Kenya, the Ministry of Health operates a number of treatment centers for substance abuse, including prescription drug abuse. These centers provide a range of services, including counseling, support groups, and medication-assisted treatment.
  • Educating the public about the risks of prescription drug abuse: In Kenya, the government and non-governmental organizations (NGOs) have undertaken efforts to raise awareness about the dangers of prescription drug abuse. This includes providing information about the risks of taking prescription drugs without a prescription, the signs of prescription drug abuse, and the importance of properly storing and disposing of prescription drugs.
  • Promoting the safe disposal of prescription drugs: In Kenya, the Pharmacy and Poisons Board has established a program for the safe disposal of prescription drugs. This program aims to reduce the risk of prescription drugs being misused or diverted to the black market.

In comparison to other forms of drug abuse, the dangers of prescription drug abuse can vary. For example, the abuse of some prescription drugs, such as opioids, can be particularly dangerous due to their high potential for overdose and addiction. However, the abuse of other drugs, such as stimulants or hallucinogens, can also have serious consequences. It is important to note that all forms of drug abuse carry risks and can have negative impacts on an individual's health and well-being. A study published in the Journal of Addiction Medicine in 2015 found that the prevalence of prescription drug abuse may be underestimated in the general population due to underreporting in surveys and other forms of self-report data. The authors of the study suggest that alternative methods, such as drug testing, may be needed to accurately assess the prevalence of prescription drug abuse in the general population. Klaine, E., Kranzler, H. R., & Calvert, G. M. (2015). Prescription drug abuse prevalence: Do self-report data underestimate the problem? Journal of Addiction Medicine, 9(6), 431-436. The prevalence of prescription drug abuse may also vary depending on the specific population being studied.
